About Sudha Murthy

Sudha Murthy is an Indian author, social worker, and philanthropist. She was born on August 19, 1950 in Shiggaon, Karnataka, India.

She is the Chairperson of the Infosys Foundation, a non-profit organization which works in the areas of healthcare, education, and rural development.

Achievements

  • Received the Padma Shri in 2006 for her contribution to literature and social work
  • Authored several books, including novels, children's books, and collections of short stories
  • Donated over 22,000 books to schools and libraries across Karnataka
  • Started the Sudha Murthy Children's Library in the city of Hubli

Education and Career

Sudha Murthy completed her engineering degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from B.V.B. College of Engineering and Technology, Hubli.

She was the first female engineer to be hired by India's largest auto manufacturer, TATA Engineering and Locomotive Company (TELCO) in Pune. She worked there as a development engineer for 7 years before resigning to pursue her passion for writing.

Infosys Foundation

The Infosys Foundation is a non-profit organization established in 1996 by Sudha Murthy and her husband, N.R. Narayana Murthy. The foundation's primary focus is on education, rural development, healthcare, arts and culture, and disaster relief.

Some of the foundation's notable initiatives include:

  • Building and maintaining schools and libraries across India
  • Providing healthcare services to underprivileged communities
  • Promoting Indian classical music and dance through sponsorships and concerts
  • Providing relief and rehabilitation to victims of natural disasters
